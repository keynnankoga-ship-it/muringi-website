CHARITY MURINGI
JABER IS JABER

A small corner of the internet dedicated to you❤️.

Home

I’ve been thinking about how to say this without sounding like a malfunctioning romantic comedy character… but unfortunately that ship has already sailed, crashed into the harbor, and is now on fire. So here we go.

My heart still owes me a formal apology for the way it completely abandoned its duties the moment I saw you at the Nairobi Food Festival. One second I was a normal, functioning human being trying to decide whether I needed another Ratata (which, let’s be honest, the answer is always yes)… and the next second you arrived and my brain just shut down like someone unplugged it from the wall. Breathing? Optional. Thinking? Impossible. Looking normal? Absolutely not.

You were so ridiculously attractive that I’m still convinced the sun dimmed itself out of professional courtesy. Somewhere up there it probably muttered, “Yeah… I’ll just let her handle the lighting today.” But the real problem started after we actually began talking. Because initially I thought the situation was simple: beautiful girl, heart goes boom, end of story. Very predictable. Very manageable.

Except then you started sharing your thoughts, and suddenly my brain had a second problem. You have this clarity when you talk about things, the way you see the world, the way you explain ideas, the way you notice details most people miss and it makes everything feel more interesting, more meaningful. It’s like you quietly turn the contrast up on life.

And then there’s your laugh. That laugh should honestly come with a warning label. It’s the kind that sneaks up on you and suddenly the whole room feels lighter. The kind that makes other people laugh even when they don’t know the joke. The kind that makes me realize, mid-conversation, that I’m smiling like an idiot. Which is deeply embarrassing for someone who once considered himself a reasonably composed adult.

Somewhere between those conversations, those laughs, and the way you casually say things that make me rethink the entire universe, something shifted. What started as “wow, she’s amazing” quietly turned into “wow… I really don’t want a world where she’s not in my life every day.” And that’s where things got complicated. Because lately, the phrase “just friends” has started to feel like a typo in the script of whatever story we’ve stumbled into. Like the universe accidentally hit backspace on a line that was supposed to say something bigger.

I find myself wanting to be the person who hears that laugh every day. The one who gets front-row seats to your thoughts, your ambitions, your random midnight ideas, and your brilliant way of making sense of the world. I want to be the person cheering for you when you chase something big… the one bringing snacks when life gets stressful… the one reminding you how incredible you are on the days you somehow forget.

Somehow, without asking my permission, you’ve become my favorite adventure. And trust me, I’ve tried to act normal about it. I really have. But my heart apparently has the emotional discipline of a golden retriever and refuses to play it cool.

So here’s the honest truth: I don’t want to keep “falling” for you like some clumsy cartoon character tripping over his own feet. I’d much rather just stand beside you and see where this goes.

Because if life is a story, I have a strong feeling that you’re not just a beautiful chapter. You’re the part I want to keep reading every single day.
